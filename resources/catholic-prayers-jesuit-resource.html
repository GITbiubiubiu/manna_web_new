The Catholic Church has a long history of formalizing doctrine and developing resources to help individuals grow in the faith. Catholic prayers and creeds are especially helpful in learning important teachings and growing in your spiritual walk.

Over the centuries, ministry leaders and friends of the Church have created guiding prayer templates—a few are included in this article. In most cases, the prayers are not specifically Catholic Prayers—that is, for Catholics only—they are helps for all who desire to share needs, concerns, and praises to God.

Prayer and personal study (discipleship) are essential keys to growing in the faith. The Catholic Prayers are organized (below) by category. We encourage you to use these sample prayers as a guide and personalize or customize them as you feel led. Remember, God is more concerned with your heart than your words. Pray with sincerity, with passion, and with precision. Pray often, and utilize the Manna App resource to maximize efforts to grow in your spiritual walk.

1. Sample Catholic Prayers for Meals

These guiding Catholic prayers are well-suited before or during meals or fellowship celebrations.

Bless us, oh Lord

Bless us, Oh Lord, and these thy gifts, which we are about to receive, from thy bounty, through Christ, Our Lord, Amen.

May all be Fed

May all be fed. May all be healed. May all be loved.

An Invocation

Loving God, bless all those gathered here today as we come together in friendship and fellowship. Thank you for the blessings of our individual and collective God-given gifts.

Place in our hearts the desire to make a difference to our families, to our community, to our country, and to the many cultures and peoples worldwide.

Give us balance in times of distraction and uncertainty. Help us move towards our goals with determination and always with an abundant sense of humor.

Thank you for food in a world where many know only hunger; for our faith in a world where many know fear; for friends in a world where many know only loneliness.

Please bless this food we are about to share, those who prepared it, those who serve it, and those who have worked to make today the special occasion that it is.

For all of this, we give You thanks.

Giving Thanks

For food in a world where many walk in hunger; for faith in a world where many walk in fear; for friends in a world where many walk alone. We give you thanks, O Lord.

Humble Hearts

In a world where so many are hungry, may we eat this food with humble hearts; in a world where so many are lonely, May we share this friendship with joyful hearts.

2. Catholic Prayers of Thanksgiving

These guiding Catholic prayers are well-suited for guiding believers in giving thanks for friends and family, special occasions and God's many blessings.

Thanks for Family and Friends

Blessed are you, loving Father, for all Your gifts to us. Blessed are You for giving us family and friends to be with us in times of joy and sorrow, to help us in days of need, and to rejoice with us in moments of celebration.

Father, we praise You for Your son Jesus, who knew the happiness of family and friends, and in the love of your Holy Spirit. Blessed are you forever and ever.

Easter Table Prayer

Creator God and Lord of Life, You who call forth from the darkness of death all those who love You. We rejoice, on this Easter Sunday, in the resurrection from the dead of our Lord, Jesus Christ.

Visit our home and this table with Your bright blessing of peace and life. We pause in the midst of this prayer to remember all the holy dead of our family who live now in You and who await the final and glorious resurrection of the dead.

May they and we, because of our faith in You, our God, taste in the victory of life over death. May the Risen Christ, our Lord and Savior, be our guest as we celebrate His resurrection with this Easter Sunday dinner.

3. Sample Catholic Prayers for Discernment and Wisdom

These guiding Catholic prayers are well-suited for those seeking guidance, clarification, and wisdom from the Lord.

A Prayer for Wisdom and Peace When Making Big Decisions

Dear heavenly Father, you number our hairs and determine our days; you hang the stars and feed the sparrows; you open doors no one can shut and shut doors no one can open. Surely, we can trust you when the time comes for making big decisions, or for that matter, any decisions.

We are in just such a season again, Father, and we know we are not alone. We will trust you for generous wisdom, straight paths, and peaceful hearts, all for your glory.

How we praise you for being the decision-making-God. It's not our decisions, but Yours that make all the difference. We will plan, but we trust You to order our steps. We will pray, but ask you to fix our prayers en route to heaven.

Deep Listening

God of silence and God of all sound, help me to listen. Help me to do the deep listening to the sounds of my soul, waiting to hear your soft voice calling me deeper into you.

Give me attentive ears that begin to separate the noise from the sounds that are You; You who have been speaking to me and through me my whole life, for so long that you can seem like background noise. Today, help me hear You anew.

Help Me To Discern

Today, O God of all days, give me an experience of your heart. Draw me deep into your very being, into the core of your love for me, others, and the world.

Give me a glimpse of others the way you see others: loving them, forgiving them, and delighting in the way they give glory to God through their very existence.

Help me to discern out of that open place of deep affection so that I too might be a useful vessel of your love in the world.

4. Unity Prayers

These guiding Catholic prayers are well-suited for those seeking information on what the Bible says about unity and compassion.

Prayer For Oneness

I rest in you, Spirit of Life. I place in you my feet, my legs, my torso, my arms, my shoulders, my head, and allow you to support all that I am. I rest in you, Spirit of Life.

I rest in you, Spirit of Love. I place in you my heart, my feelings, my emotions, my passions, my desires, my loves, and allow you to hold all that I am. I rest in you, Spirit of Love.

I rest in you, Spirit of Wisdom. I place in you my mind, my thoughts, my questions, my wonderings, my knowings, my doubts, and allow you to embrace all that I am. I rest in you, Spirit of Wisdom.

I rest in you, Spirit of Compassion. I place in you my will, my choices, my actions, my hopes, my dreams, my fears, and allow you to guide all that I am. I rest in you, Spirit of Compassion.

I rest in you, Spirit of Unity. I place in you my relationships, my community, my world, my cosmos, my sense of connection with all that is, and allow you to weave together all that I am with all that is. I rest in you, Spirit of Unity.

I rest in you, Spirit of Mystery. I place in you all that I cannot name, cannot understand, cannot grasp, cannot control, and allow you to hold in your vastness all that I am and all that I am not. I rest in you, Spirit of Mystery.

I rest in you, and I am at peace.

5. Meeting and Academic Prayers

These guiding Catholic prayers are well-suited for those seeking guidance for meetings, academic endeavors, and group activities.

Prayer for Meetings

Loving God, as we gather for this meeting, we ask for your guidance and wisdom. Help us to listen to one another with open hearts and minds. Grant us the courage to speak truthfully and the humility to receive feedback gracefully.

May our discussions be productive and our decisions be made with the common good in mind. Help us to work together in harmony, respecting our differences and building on our shared values.

We ask for your blessing on our time together and on the work that flows from our gathering. May all that we do serve your greater glory and the good of those we serve.

Academic Prayer

God of all knowledge and wisdom, we come before you as students and seekers of truth. Open our minds to new understanding and our hearts to the wonder of learning.

Grant us the discipline to study diligently, the patience to work through challenges, and the humility to ask for help when we need it. Help us to use our education not just for personal advancement, but to serve others and make a positive difference in the world.

Bless our teachers and mentors who guide us on this journey of discovery. May we always remember that true wisdom begins with reverence for you.

6. Creation Prayers

These guiding Catholic prayers are well-suited for those seeking to connect with God through His creation and to pray for environmental stewardship.

Prayer for Creation

Creator God, we marvel at the beauty and complexity of your creation. From the vastness of the cosmos to the intricate details of a single flower, everything speaks of your love and wisdom.

Help us to be faithful stewards of the earth you have entrusted to our care. Give us the wisdom to live in harmony with nature and the courage to protect the environment for future generations.

May we see your presence in all of creation and respond with gratitude, wonder, and responsible action. Help us to remember that we are part of your creation, called to care for our common home.

Seasonal Prayer

God of all seasons, we thank you for the rhythm of time that marks our days and years. In spring, we celebrate new life and fresh beginnings. In summer, we enjoy abundance and growth. In autumn, we gather the harvest and prepare for rest. In winter, we find peace in stillness and reflection.

Help us to embrace each season of our lives with trust in your providence. May we find you in times of growth and in times of dormancy, in moments of abundance and in periods of scarcity.

7. Family Prayers

These guiding Catholic prayers are well-suited for families seeking to pray together and strengthen their bonds of love.

Family Unity Prayer

Loving God, you have blessed us with the gift of family. Help us to cherish one another and to grow in love and understanding. When we disagree, help us to listen with patience and speak with kindness.

Strengthen the bonds that unite us and help us to support one another through all of life's joys and challenges. May our home be a place of peace, laughter, and unconditional love.

Bless our family members who are far away, and keep us connected in heart even when we are apart. Help us to be a source of blessing to others and to share your love with all we meet.

Prayer for Children

Heavenly Father, we entrust our children to your loving care. Watch over them as they grow and learn. Protect them from harm and guide them in the paths of righteousness.

Help us as parents to be good examples of your love and to teach our children your ways. Give us wisdom in our guidance and patience in our instruction.

May our children come to know you personally and find their purpose in serving you and others. Bless them with health, happiness, and the knowledge of your unfailing love.

8. Thanksgiving Prayers

These guiding Catholic prayers are well-suited for expressing gratitude to God for His many blessings.

General Thanksgiving

Gracious God, we come before you with hearts full of gratitude. Thank you for the gift of life, for health and strength, for family and friends, for food and shelter.

Thank you for your constant presence in our lives, for your guidance in times of uncertainty, and for your comfort in times of sorrow. Help us to never take your blessings for granted.

May our gratitude move us to generous action, sharing your blessings with others and working to build a more just and compassionate world.

Thanksgiving for Challenges

God of all circumstances, we thank you not only for the good times but also for the challenges that help us grow. Thank you for the difficulties that teach us patience, the setbacks that build our resilience, and the struggles that deepen our faith.

Help us to see your hand at work even in the midst of trials. Give us the grace to learn from every experience and to emerge stronger and more compassionate.

9. Forgiveness Prayers

These guiding Catholic prayers are well-suited for those seeking to forgive others or to receive forgiveness.

Prayer for Forgiveness

Merciful God, you know the hurts we carry and the resentments that burden our hearts. Help us to forgive those who have wronged us, just as you have forgiven us.

Give us the strength to let go of anger and the wisdom to see others through your eyes of compassion. Heal the wounds in our relationships and restore broken bonds where possible.

Help us also to forgive ourselves for our own failures and mistakes. May your mercy wash over us and give us a fresh start.

Seeking Forgiveness

Loving Father, we come before you acknowledging our sins and failures. We have fallen short of your glory and have hurt others through our words and actions.

We are truly sorry for our sins and ask for your forgiveness. Help us to make amends where possible and to change our ways going forward.

Thank you for your mercy that is new every morning and for the gift of redemption through your Son, Jesus Christ.

10. Gratitude Prayers

These guiding Catholic prayers are well-suited for cultivating a heart of gratitude in daily life.

Daily Gratitude

Thank you, God, for this new day and for all the possibilities it holds. Thank you for the gift of sight to see your beauty, for ears to hear your voice, for hands to serve others, and for feet to walk in your ways.

Thank you for the simple pleasures of life: a warm cup of coffee, a friend's smile, a child's laughter, a beautiful sunset. Help us to notice and appreciate these small gifts throughout our day.

May our hearts be always filled with gratitude, and may our lives be a continuous prayer of thanksgiving to you.

Evening Gratitude

As this day comes to an end, we pause to thank you, God, for all the ways you have blessed us. Thank you for moments of joy, for challenges that helped us grow, and for your constant presence with us.

Forgive us for the times we failed to notice your goodness or to respond with gratitude. Help us to rest peacefully in your love and to wake tomorrow with hearts ready to serve you.

We entrust this night to your care and look forward to the new mercies that await us in the morning.

Note: These prayers are meant to be guides and starting points for your own personal prayer life. Feel free to adapt them to your specific needs and circumstances. Remember that God desires authentic communication from the heart above all else.
